Hey there, internet friends!

Ilia and Pete are doing research on our media consumption habits. Neither of us care about your tinder profile pics, nor about any of your personal details — all we want to do is get a sense of the media pages y'all follow.

This isn't going out to marketers (ugh!) or being monetized (money — the worst!). All we want to do is find out which news organizations our friendship network follows on Facebook.

Since this isn't our first rodeo, here's a privacy policy for anyone who wants to nerd out about that.

Privacy Policy

This is the main privacy policy for Balance.

We collect information about your Facebook page likes in order to determine:

  1. The breadth of news consumption among Facebook users.

  2. The media consumption patterns among clusters of Facebook users.

Our principles

  1. We do our very best to protect your privacy by using security technology appropriately.

  2. We will respect your privacy.

  3. We will be clear in our dealings with you as to what information about you we will collect and how we will use it.

  4. We will use personal information only for the purposes for which it was originally collected and we will make sure we delete it securely.

What information do we collect?

We collect the "liked" pages linked to your Facebook account.

Who we share data with

We will not share your personal information with others for marketing purposes, but may use an anonymized and aggregated compilation of the total list of news organizations as part of a broader, publicly available research program, as well as grant funding applications.

Use of Cookies

We don't use cookies.

Advertisement

None.
